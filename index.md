## The common pattern travelers describe

1. Strong online reviews build trust.
2. A professional-looking contract and a request for payment up front.
3. Pressure or requests to pay the full amount earlier than the schedule would suggest.
4. A portion of the meals are delivered well.
5. An "accident" or emergency is cited, and service stops.
6. A refund is promised for a future date, then does not arrive.
7. Communication channels go quiet or are blocked.

## For other travelers (general due-diligence tips)

- Prefer **credit card** payment, which allows a chargeback if services are not delivered. Avoid Wise, bank transfer, SINPE, or cash for large prepayments, as these are very difficult to reverse.
- Do not pay the full amount up front. Tie payments to meals actually delivered.
- Book through a platform or property manager that offers a clear cancellation and refund policy.
- Keep written records of your contract, all messages, and every payment.
